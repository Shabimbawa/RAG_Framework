Item 1.01    Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement.

As previously disclosed, on January 9, 2025, AEP Transmission Company, LLC (“AEP Transmission”), a wholly owned subsidiary of American Electric Power Company, Inc. (“AEP”), along with Midwest Transmission Holdings, LLC (the “Company”), a newly formed wholly owned subsidiary of AEP Transmission that owns all of the issued and outstanding stock of each of AEP Indiana Michigan Transmission Company, Inc., and AEP Ohio Transmission Company, Inc., entered into a Contribution Agreement (the “Contribution Agreement”) with Olympus BidCo L.P. (“Investor”), a special purpose entity controlled by (i) investment funds managed by or affiliated with Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co. L.P. and (ii) Public Sector Pension Investment Board, pursuant to which the Company agreed to issue to Investor at the closing (the “Closing”), and Investor agreed to acquire from the Company, certain newly issued membership interests of the Company, such that Investor will own 19.9% of the issued and outstanding membership interests of the Company, for a purchase price of $2,820,000,000. The parties to the Contribution Agreement consummated the Closing on June 5, 2025.

On June 5, 2025, pursuant to the terms of the Contribution Agreement and in connection with the Closing, Investor, the Company and AEP Transmission entered into an Amended and Restated Limited Liability Company Agreement of the Company (the “LLC Agreement”). The LLC Agreement is effective from and after the Closing, and contains rights and obligations of the Company, AEP Transmission and Investor pertaining to the governance of the Company, certain transfer restrictions and other transfer rights with respect to the Company’s equity, capital contributions to and distributions from the Company, significant corporate events involving the Company and its subsidiaries (including certain investor protections, including, among other things, requiring Investor approval for the Company to take certain major actions), and other similar matters affecting AEP Transmission’s and Investor’s respective interests in the Company. Under the LLC Agreement, Investor is entitled to appoint two directors to the board of managers of the Company (the “Board”) so long as Investor (together with its Affiliates (as defined in the LLC Agreement)) holds at least a 17.5% Percentage Interest (as defined in the LLC Agreement). In connection with the Closing, Investor appointed two managers to the Board, such that the Board is now comprised of eleven directors, two appointed by Investor and nine appointed by AEP Transmission.
