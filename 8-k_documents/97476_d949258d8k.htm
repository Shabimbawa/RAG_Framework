ITEM 5.03. Amendments to Articles of Incorporation or Bylaws; Change in Fiscal Year

On July 16, 2020, the Board of Directors of Texas Instruments Incorporated (the “Company”) approved By-Law amendments to clarify annual meeting practices and clarify certain governance practices in rare circumstances like a pandemic, and to align with developments in Delaware law and address other administrative matters.

Article II, Sections 1 and 3-5 have been updated to remove the default date and location of the annual stockholders meeting and to clarify the Company’s ability to hold the meeting virtually and to adjourn, postpone, and establish rules of order for the meeting.

Article II, Sections 9 and 10 have been updated to require disclosure of record ownership and other information when submitting nominees and other proposals outside of the Exchange Act Rule 14a-8 process.

Article III, Sections 2-4 and 7-9 have been updated to refresh outdated board practices, including to clarify governance procedures in rare circumstances like a pandemic, to no longer distinguish an annual board meeting from a regular board meeting, and to remove the default number of directors.    

Article IV, Section 1 was updated to mirror Delaware law regarding stock certificate issuances.

Other technical and administrative changes were also approved by the Board of Directors, including changes to clarify that electronic communications are considered written.

The foregoing description of the Company’s By-Laws is qualified in all respects by reference to the text of the By-Laws, which are attached hereto as Exhibit 3 and incorporated herein by reference.
